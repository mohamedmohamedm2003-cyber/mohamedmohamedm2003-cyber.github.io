Final Year Research — Geotechnics

The Effect of Curing Time on CBR Value Using Bacteria (Bacillus Tropicus)

Conducted a full geotechnical laboratory research program investigating how bacterial stabilization using Bacillus Tropicus affects the California Bearing Ratio (CBR) of soil over varying curing times — evaluating a bio-cementation approach to subgrade strength improvement.

Compared soaked vs. unsoaked soil behavior and analyzed how different curing durations influence the structural capacity of stabilized subgrade — contributing to emerging research in sustainable geotechnical engineering.
